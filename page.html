Who We Are

The Serenity Project was founded by and is made up of women who are passionate about helping survivors love themselves, become confident in their own skin, and write their own story.

"Beauty Does not come with one story"

About Us

Suicide is among the leading causes of death for at-risk women. We focus our efforts on women survivors of trauma (including domestic violence, acid attacks, and body dysmorphia). With mental health stigmatization, societal shaming of survivors, and a lack of tools for at-risk women to gain confidence, we aim to enrich and save the lives of women in our project. These women deserve to be celebrated for their perseverance and invested in for their future.

Serene Singh

Serene made TSP after losing a best friend, a survivor, to suicide. As a model, she saw the effect of unhealthy beauty ideals, but also, the power runways have to grow confidence. While working in the US Senate on violence, trafficking, and rape policies she noticed womxn survivors were never in-charge of the settings where changes impacting survivors were created. Serene dreamt of empowering survivors to dream again. Transforming pain into power. A former America’s Junior Miss and Miss Colorado Teen, Singh’s pageantry and modeling experience inspires her to empower for women worldwide, which has led her to create The Serenity Project nonprofit to give confidence tools to at-risk women, work for Mrs. Obama and the Girls Opportunity Alliance to improve girls’ education, win Victoria Secret’s GRL PWR campaign, and serve as a Dalai Lama Fellow, Global Changemaker, and Clinton Global Initiative Scholar.

What We Do

We empower at-risk women by challenging unhealthy beauty standards, the rising number of suicide attempts, and the lack of support, tools, and skills survivors receive to grow through their trauma.
